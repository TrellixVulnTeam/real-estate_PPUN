Intro




This website provides information on what things to take into consideration when it comes to investing in a residential property. In addition, it contains an excerpt of an interview done by someone who excelled in this field. Read through this website, and I hope you find it helpful!

Article


Residential real estate refers to properties used for residential purposes. When it comes to residential real estate, there are two things to keep in mind: The location of the property and its expenses. This article will go into detail about various things to look for in a profitable residential property.


The location in which the property resides will determine the type of tenants and the vacant rate. If you buy properties near a university, students may struggle to pay rent, and there would be vacancies during the summer. However, properties near a post-secondary institution and growing employment opportunities attract tenants. Crimes deter tenets. As such, it's good to research crime statistics, the rate of vandalism, and the frequency of police presence. Beyond that, look for properties with a lot of construction as it shows potential for future development.


How much money could you potentially make or lose from your investment? First, let's take in account of insurance. Were natural disasters to arise, the insurance coverage will eat away at your rental income. As such, look for places with a low risk of fires, earthquakes or floods. Next, check whether property tax increases are probable in the future. This is a great indicator that financial distress is at play, and you will potentially lose money to vacancies.


In conclusion, look for properties that will support amenities, employment opportunities, and education. Ensure that any property income can cover the mortgage payment, taxes, and other expenses in the near future. Most importantly, know that even if you make money now; you can lose money at any time. Like any investment, there's no grantee.

The interview process was long. I spent the majority of time asking questions within questions. It was more of a casual conversation than an interview. While I did not learn anything new, it was fun.
